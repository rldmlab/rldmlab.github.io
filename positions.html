Working Together at the CCN lab

In our lab, open and frequent conversations are something we truly value. Everyone should feel comfortable sharing ideas, asking questions, and offering perspectives—no matter how early or rough they may be.

We care deeply about creating a collaborative environment where curiosity, honest feedback, and mutual support are part of our everyday interactions. We grow as scientists when we’re open to being challenged and generous in helping each other improve.

Each person leads their own project and takes ownership of their work. At the same time, we’re all working toward broader questions that tie our projects together. That’s why it’s important for everyone to stay curious about what others are doing and be open to giving and receiving input across projects.

As a mentor, I’m here to support you, especially when things get tough. You can always come to me with questions, ideas, or concerns, and I’ll do my best to listen and help.

I fully support a range of career paths—both academic and non-academic—and I’m happy to offer my full support in helping you get there. What matters most is that you stay curious and committed to doing thoughtful, high-quality science during your time in the lab. Your long-term goals may shape how you prioritize your work, but I believe that maintaining strong research standards will benefit you no matter where your path leads.

If you join the team, I hope we can build a community together—one where people feel comfortable exploring meaningful questions, learning through the process, and sharing the journey with others who are curious and excited about the work.

A Postdoctoral Researcher ▼

Computational Cognitive Neuroscience Lab
Department of Psychology, Virginia Tech

The CCN Lab is seeking a highly motivated Postdoctoral Researcher to join our team investigating the neural and computational mechanisms of structured learning and flexible cognition.

About the Lab

Our research combines behavioral experiments, neuroimaging (fMRI, OPM-MEG), and computational modeling to understand how the human brain builds internal models of task structure, transfers knowledge across contexts, and generalizes to novel problem-solving.

Project Focus
  • Neural Representations of Cognitive Maps and Structural Relational Knowledge
  • Neural Computations Underlying Abstraction and Cross-Task Generalization
  • Neural Representations of Others’ Minds During Social Interaction
Responsibilities
  • Design and run behavioral and neuroimaging experiments
  • Develop and analyze computational models
  • Analyze neuroimaging (fMRI and/or OPM-MEG) data
  • Prepare publications and present findings
  • Mentor graduate and undergraduate students in the lab
Preferred Qualifications

(Please address each in your application)

  • Ph.D. in a relevant field (e.g., cognitive neuroscience, psychology, computer science, or a related discipline)
  • Programming experience (e.g., Python, MATLAB, or R)
  • Experience with neuroimaging, computational modeling, and/or machine learning
  • Publication record
  • Strong communication skills

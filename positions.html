A Postdoctoral Researcher

The RLDM Lab is seeking a highly motivated Postdoctoral Researcher to join our interdisciplinary team investigating the neural and computational mechanisms of structured learning and flexible cognition.

About the Lab

Our research combines behavioral experiments, neuroimaging (fMRI, OPM-MEG), and computational modeling to understand how the human brain builds internal models of task structure, transfers knowledge across contexts, and adapts to changing environments. We aim to bridge cognitive neuroscience with machine learning to uncover principles that support human generalization and decision-making.

Project Focus

  • Curriculum learning and knowledge transfer
  • Neural representation of abstract structure and task hierarchies
  • Cognitive map formation and structural generalization
  • Replay and latent structure inference using fMRI and MEG

Responsibilities

  • Design and run behavioral and neuroimaging experiments (fMRI, OPM-MEG)
  • Develop and analyze computational models (e.g., reinforcement learning, Bayesian inference, graph-based representations)
  • Preprocess and analyze neuroimaging data
  • Write manuscripts and present findings at scientific conferences
  • Mentor graduate and undergraduate students

Required Qualifications

  • Ph.D. in Cognitive Neuroscience, Psychology, Computer Science, Biomedical Engineering, or a related field
  • Strong programming skills (Python, MATLAB, or similar)
  • Experience in neuroimaging, computational modeling, or machine learning
  • Demonstrated ability to publish scientific research
  • Excellent communication and collaborative skills

Preferred Qualifications

  • Background in structured cognition, learning, or decision-making
  • Familiarity with neuroimaging pipelines (fMRIPrep, MNE, AFNI, or SPM)
  • Interest in cognitive theories of transfer and generalization

Location & Appointment

The lab is located in the Department of Psychology at Virginia Tech (Blacksburg, VA). This is a 1-year appointment with the possibility of renewal based on performance and funding. Start date is flexible. Applications will be reviewed on a rolling basis.

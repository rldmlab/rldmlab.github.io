About

The Computational Cognitive Neuroscience Lab, directed by Dr. Seongmin A. Park, investigates the computational principles by which the human brain supports intelligence, learning, and flexible decision-making. Our core question centers on how the brain extracts abstract knowledge from diverse experiences and generalizes that knowledge to guide behavior in novel situations. We explore how the brain builds internal models of the worldâ€”structured representations that enable reasoning, prediction, and goal-directed action. Our research combines behavioral experiments, neuroimaging techniques (fMRI, OPM-MEG, and EEG), and computational modeling, including both normative models (e.g., Bayesian Learning, Reinforcement Learning) and Neural Networks. By integrating approaches from psychology, neuroscience, machine learning, and behavioral economics, we aim to understand how the brain represents concepts, learns structural relationships, and makes novel inferences.
